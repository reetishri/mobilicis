About Japan

Culture

Japan boasts a rich cultural heritage spanning centuries, from traditional arts such as tea ceremonies and kabuki theater to modern pop culture phenomena like anime and manga.

Economy

Japan is a global economic powerhouse, known for its advanced technology, automotive industry, and strong export-oriented economy. Tokyo, the capital city, is a major financial center.

Work Environment

Japanese work culture emphasizes dedication, teamwork, and attention to detail. Companies often prioritize long-term employment and offer benefits like bonuses and career development opportunities.

Business Culture

Japanese business culture places a strong emphasis on hierarchy, respect, and harmony. Business meetings often start with exchanging business cards (meishi) and bowing as a sign of respect.

Living in Japan

Living in Japan offers a unique blend of traditional and modern lifestyles. From bustling city centers to serene rural areas, Japan provides diverse living environments with excellent infrastructure and safety.

Visa Requirements

Visa requirements for working in Japan vary depending on factors such as nationality, type of employment, and intended duration of stay. Common visa types include work visas, student visas, and working holiday visas.
